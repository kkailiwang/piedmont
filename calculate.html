State of Black Education Oakland
State of Black Education’s mission is to create equitable space to reflect on Oakland’s Black community’s experiences with education in order to create and advocate for informed policy demands. Current efforts include pushing for more Black educators in Oakland schools, raising awareness of the fact that most Black graduates in OUSD do not meet A-G requirements for 4-year universities, and urging the district to use excess property to help house teachers who cannot afford to live in Oakland. Past campaigns include the Opportunity Ticket, through which the organization helped pass a policy that gives priority to parents to select the school of their choice if their school happens to close down.

Planting Justice
The Planting Justice Education Program activates people most directly impacted by poverty and food injustice to create a more local and sustainable food system by developing their skills in ecological design, nutrition education, and multimedia arts. The work of the Education program takes place at high schools in East Oakland. Planting Justice hires staff directly from the communities they serve, and develops important mentorship opportunities with our students through consistent weekly programming. Planting Justice Educators and community allies have developed the Plant! Cook! Organize! curriculum, available online soon.

Black Organizing Project
Black Organizing Project is a Black member-led community organization reviving the spirit of Oakland’s Black community through relationship building, leadership development, political education and policy-change. Through their Bettering Our Schools System (B.O.S.S) Campaign started in 2011, the vision is to end the criminalization of Black and Brown students in the Oakland Unified School District; Invest in alternative models of school safety that don’t rely on police; And to restructure the current policies and practices within OUSD that don’t take a restorative justice approach to student discipline and promotes the school to prison pipeline. They are calling for the immediate removal of the Oakland Unified School police department. On June 24, 2020, OUSD will be voting on thier George Floyd Resolution to eliminate OSPD. If the board votes in favor of the resolution, Oakland will be the first district in the nation to eliminate an entire armed school police department.
