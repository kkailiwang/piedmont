About Us

#popthebubble
It is common to hear remarks on how us Piedmonters live in “such a bubble”, referencing the tight-knit and highly involved nature of this small community.

But the bubble that exists around Piedmont is not just metaphorical. There is a concrete difference between the racial and economic makeup of those inside the “bubble” and those in the community directly surrounding it. But these differences have ramifications beyond a lack of diversity. It’s important to acknowledge the concrete, systemic inequalities that exist in our own community of Oakland.

Inspired to take long-term action to solve issues that deeply affect our community, we've created POPS, a website that serves as a long-term hub for Piedmont to centralize our donations to three Oakland based organizations that work to empower black youth and the Oakland School systems.

We envision this hub as a place where Piedmont can come together to show our Oakland community the same care we show Piedmont.

Here, you can also find resources about inequities present in Oakland schools to raise awareness about what our own community can do to bring justice to Oakland Public Schools.

We also hope to use this platform to share resources and student voices that discuss racism within Piedmont, as we want to acknowledge there is work to be done within our community, too. We hope that our donations and awareness can help breakdown the historic systematic educational divide between Oakland and Piedmont.

The organizations we are supporting are the Black Organizing Project, Planting Justice, and The State of Black Education Oakland. They are all focused on improving the state of Oakland Public education through a nutritional focus, educator support programs, and policy for combatting the school to prison pipeline.

Activism has long been regarded as more of a moment than a movement. With this initiative, we hope to start Piedmont’s journey toward long-lasting change.
